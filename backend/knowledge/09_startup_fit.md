# Startup Fit

## Summary

Ankit is a strong fit for startups because he has already worked in early-stage environments and has shipped product areas with high ownership.

## Startup traits

Ankit brings:

- High ownership.
- Comfort with ambiguity.
- Full-stack execution.
- Product thinking.
- Startup pace experience.
- Ability to work with cross-functional teams.
- Willingness to own unglamorous but important workflows.

## Evidence from Pulley

At Pulley, Ankit worked as an early engineer. This matters because early engineers need to do more than code. They must understand the business, support product changes, debug customer issues, and ship without perfect specifications.

Strong examples:

- Sole engineer on Offer Letter Builder.
- Work on valuation workflows.
- Work on communication systems.
- Contribution across frontend, backend, and internal workflows.

## Evidence from Mochi Health

At Mochi Health, Ankit works on healthcare operations systems. This requires practical judgment because mistakes in internal workflows can create real operational problems.

Strong examples:

- Provider license-expiry redesign.
- Collaborating physician workflows.
- Provider availability monitoring.
- Credentialing tools.

## Good founder question answers

### Can Ankit work without detailed specs?

Yes. His strongest examples involve messy workflows where the product problem needed shaping.

### Is Ankit only a frontend engineer?

No. He is frontend-strong, but his work spans full-stack product systems, backend workflows, PostgreSQL-backed features, and operational tooling.

### Is Ankit a fit for AI startups?

He is a strong fit for AI product engineering roles where the job is to build useful AI-enabled workflows. He should not be positioned as a deep ML researcher.

### What type of startup should hire Ankit?

A startup building SaaS, internal tools, workflow automation, AI-assisted operations, healthcare operations, equity/finance workflows, or product-led B2B software.
